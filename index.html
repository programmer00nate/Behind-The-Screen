When being “connected” still feels lonely

Online chats can be comforting — but sometimes they feel shallow, scripted, or emotionally distant. This project collects stories, art, and practical tips to help people find more meaningful human connection both online and off.

Trigger warning: this site contains personal stories about loneliness and mental health. If you're in crisis, please contact local emergency services or the suicide/crisis hotline in your country.

Shared Stories

What we learned

Small actions can change how connected someone feels: active listening, asking open questions, checking in offline, and creating spaces where people feel safe to share. This project focuses on awareness, visual expression, and community engagement.

Share your story

Add a short anonymous story about a time you felt lonely online. No personal names. We display entries here after submission.

Resources & tips

  • Active listening: ask one follow-up question before giving advice.
  • Check-ins: be specific: “How are you today, really?”
  • Offline invitations: Turn online chats into voice notes or meet-ups where possible.
  • Boundaries: it's okay to step away to protect your mental health.
  • Safe spaces: use trigger warnings and opt-in story sharing.
